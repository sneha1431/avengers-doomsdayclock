Every MCU Post-Credits Scene That Sets Up Avengers: Doomsday

March 30, 2026 · 8 scenes covered · 10 min read

With Avengers: Doomsday arriving December 18, 2026, the MCU has spent years laying the groundwork. From Shang-Chi's mysterious beacon to the Thunderbolts post-credits scene shot on the actual Doomsday set — here is every post-credits scene that directly connects to what's coming, ranked by importance.

Scene 01

Thunderbolts — The Most Important Setup

Thunderbolts* (2025) Post-Credits

The Thunderbolts post-credits scene is the most significant Doomsday setup in the entire MCU — because it was directed by the Russo Brothers on the actual Doomsday set. Thunderbolts director Jake Schreier confirmed this. It may literally be the opening scene of Doomsday.

The scene takes place 14 months after the events of Thunderbolts. The team has rebranded. They're now called the New Avengers, operating out of the old Avengers Tower — now renamed the Watchtower. New uniforms, new branding, and an ongoing legal dispute with Sam Wilson over who gets to use the Avengers name.

Yelena Belova mentions they're being kept in the dark about a space crisis. Something is happening, something large, and nobody is telling them about it. Then the alarm goes off. An extra-dimensional ship enters Earth's orbit.

The New Avengers look up and see it — the Fantastic Four's spaceship arriving from Earth-838, crossing into the main MCU timeline. Their theme music plays. The scene ends.

This one scene sets up three major storylines for Doomsday: the New Avengers versus Sam Wilson's Avengers competing for the same name, the Fantastic Four's arrival — they're not here by choice, they're chasing Doctor Doom — and the unspecified space crisis nobody is willing to talk about. Given that the Russos shot it on the Doomsday set, there is a real possibility this scene plays out again at the start of the film.

Scene 02

Fantastic Four: First Steps — Doom Approaches Franklin

Fantastic Four: First Steps (2025) Mid-Credits

The Fantastic Four mid-credits scene is terrifying in how quietly it delivers its threat. The film ends on a celebration — Reed Richards, Sue Storm, Johnny Storm, and Ben Grimm have defeated Galactus and saved their world, Earth-838. Then the mid-credits scene begins.

Sue Storm is reading to Franklin Richards, her four-year-old son — one of the most powerful beings in the entire multiverse, a child who can create and destroy universes. She steps away to find another book. When she returns, there is someone else in the room.

A cloaked figure crouches in front of Franklin. Sue's powers activate immediately — force fields up, ready to defend her son. But Franklin is not scared. He reaches out his hand. The figure holds up a mask. Doctor Doom's mask. Robert Downey Jr.'s Doctor Doom, on Earth-838, in the Fantastic Four's own reality, approaching their child.

Why Franklin? Because he is a reality warper. He can create and destroy universes with a thought. And Doctor Doom wants that power.

The working theory: Doom steals Franklin Richards, takes him across dimensions, and the Fantastic Four follow. They chase him from Earth-838 into Earth-616. That is the ship the New Avengers see in the Thunderbolts post-credits scene. The Fantastic Four are not arriving — they are pursuing. This scene confirms Doom's plan is multiverse-wide, not limited to a single Earth.

Scene 03

The Marvels — The X-Men Enter the MCU

The Marvels (2023) Post-Credits

Monica Rambeau sacrifices herself at the end of The Marvels. She closes a tear in reality — stopping an incursion, preventing two universes from collapsing into each other — but gets trapped on the other side in the process. When she wakes up in a hospital bed, her mother is sitting next to her.

Except it is not her mother. It is a variant. Maria Rambeau from a parallel universe. Not an Air Force pilot in this reality — she is Binary, a cosmic-powered hero in red and white. And then Beast walks in. Kelsey Grammer's Hank McCoy from Fox's X-Men films. Blue fur, intelligent, working with Charles Xavier. He examines Monica and confirms she crossed through a tear in spacetime.

"She's in a reality parallel to her own — which, as Beast says, is impossible. Except it's not, because it just happened."

This scene does two things for Doomsday. First, it officially introduces the Fox X-Men into MCU continuity. Patrick Stewart, Ian McKellen, James Marsden, Alan Cumming, and Kelsey Grammer are all confirmed for Doomsday — this scene explains how they get there. Monica caused the tear. The incursion created a connection between universes, and that connection brings the X-Men in.

Second, it establishes Monica as a bridge character — a hero from Earth-616 stranded in the X-Men's reality, with Charles Xavier aware of her existence. The only way Monica gets home is if someone from Earth-616 opens another portal — or if the multiverse collapses. Which is exactly what Doctor Doom is planning.

Scene 04

Captain America: Brave New World — The Warning

Captain America: Brave New World (2025) Post-Credits

Sam Wilson visits the Raft — the underwater prison for superpowered criminals. He is there to see Samuel Sterns, the Leader. Tim Blake Nelson returning after 17 years since The Incredible Hulk. Sterns is locked up, gamma-irradiated, his brain hideously enlarged, and far too intelligent for anyone's comfort.

He has been calculating probabilities, analyzing data, and he has seen what is coming. His warning to Sam is direct:

"Do you think this is the only world? All you heroes protecting this world — do you think you're the only ones? Do you think this is the only world? We'll see what happens when you have to protect this place from the others."

The others. Plural. Not one threat — multiple threats, from multiple worlds. This is the first time Sam Wilson hears about the multiverse in any meaningful way. He was not in No Way Home. He was not in Multiverse of Madness. He has been focused entirely on Earth-616 and proving himself worthy of the shield.

Now Sterns is telling him the real fight is coming and it is not from his world. This scene is Sam's equivalent of Tony Stark's nightmare in Age of Ultron — the moment that forces a hero to think bigger and start preparing. Sam needs to reassemble the Avengers. Because the others are coming. Doomsday will prove the Leader right.

Scene 05

Shang-Chi — The Beacon Nobody Can Explain

Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings (2021) Post-Credits

September 2021. Before Kang. Before Doom was announced. Before anyone knew where Phase 6 was heading. Shang-Chi meets with Wong, Bruce Banner, and Captain Marvel — all analyzing the Ten Rings, the ancient weapons he inherited from his father.

What they discover is unsettling. The rings are sending a beacon. A signal, pulsing outward toward somewhere — or someone. Wong confirms the rings are not from Earth. Bruce Banner cannot identify the material they are made from. Captain Marvel has never seen anything like them in her travels across the galaxy.

The origin of the Ten Rings remains one of the MCU's biggest open mysteries, and it almost certainly will not be resolved until Shang-Chi 2 — but the scene matters for Doomsday for a different reason. It establishes the team. Shang-Chi, Wong, Captain Marvel, Bruce Banner — all confirmed for Avengers: Doomsday — having their first meeting together, years before the crisis.

The scene shows that even after the Avengers formally disbanded following Endgame, the infrastructure was still there — heroes staying in contact, sharing information, ready to reassemble. Sam Wilson does not have to start from scratch. The network already exists.

The beacon from the Ten Rings is still unexplained as of 2026. Whether it connects to the Beyonders, to Doom, or to something else entirely — it is a thread that has not been pulled yet. And in the MCU, threads always come back.

Scene 06

Loki Season 2 Finale — The Lynch Pin of Everything

Loki Season 2 (2023) Season Finale

Loki Season 2 does not end with a post-credits scene. The finale itself is the setup — arguably the most important single piece of Multiverse Saga storytelling outside of the Avengers films.

Loki sacrifices himself. He destroys the Temporal Loom, grabs the dying timelines with his bare hands, and weaves them back together — creating a new multiverse tree with himself at the center. He sits on a throne at the end of time, alone, maintaining every timeline, every reality, every universe. Without him, the entire multiverse collapses.

Tom Hiddleston's Loki is confirmed for Avengers: Doomsday. But how does he appear if he is stuck at the end of time holding everything together? The answer is the dramatic engine of Doomsday itself. Doom's incursions are threatening the multiverse so severely that Loki's grip is slipping.

One theory: Doom's plan is not just to conquer Earth-616 — it is to replace Loki. Take his throne. Control the multiverse from the end of time. Franklin Richards' reality-warping power would make it possible.

Loki holding the multiverse is why the Multiverse Saga exists. Without him, every reality collapses. Doomsday happens because the cracks are forming and Doom is exploiting them. Secret Wars happens because Loki can no longer hold it. The multiverse dies, and Battleworld is born from the fragments.

Scene 07

Doctor Strange: Multiverse of Madness — The Word That Changes Everything

Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness (2022) Post-Credits

The Doctor Strange post-credits scene does not tease a specific Doomsday character or plot point. It introduces a concept — and that concept is the foundation of everything coming. Incursions.

Clea appears — Charlize Theron, a sorcerer from the Dark Dimension. She tells Strange that his actions have caused an incursion: a tear between realities, two universes beginning to collapse into each other. She needs his help to fix it. Strange follows her into the Dark Dimension, his third eye open, and the film ends.

This is the first time the MCU uses the word "incursion" in the context of the multiverse — and it is the mechanic that drives everything in Phase 6. In the source comics, incursions happen when two universes occupy the same space. Both are destroyed unless one universe eliminates the other first. Doctor Doom uses the fragments of incursion-destroyed realities to build Battleworld.

The Marvels showed Monica Rambeau stopping an incursion. But stopping it and preventing more are two different things. They are accelerating, and Doomsday is where the MCU stops pretending otherwise.

Scene 08

Deadpool & Wolverine — The Void Sets the Stage

Deadpool & Wolverine (2024) Film Itself

Deadpool and Wolverine does not have a traditional post-credits scene pointing directly at Doomsday. The entire film is the setup. It takes place in the Void — the wasteland at the end of time where the TVA discards pruned timelines. A graveyard of forgotten realities.

The film shows the Fox X-Men universe, the Rami Spider-Man universe, the Blade universe — all pruned, all discarded, all piled together in one place. Leaked plot details suggest Doctor Doom creates Battleworld inside the Void. He does not build from scratch — he takes the fragments of destroyed realities already there and stitches them together into domains. Deadpool and Wolverine showed us what raw material he has to work with.

Ryan Reynolds' Deadpool is confirmed for Doomsday alongside Hugh Jackman's Wolverine, Tom Holland's Spider-Man, and reportedly Tobey Maguire's Spider-Man — all part of a ragtag Avengers lineup assembled by circumstance rather than design.

The Void is not just a setting. It is the location where the Multiverse Saga ends and Secret Wars begins. Doomsday will show what Doom does with those fragments. Secret Wars will show the result: Battleworld, built from the bones of dead realities, with Doom sitting at the top of it all.

The Full Picture

Every one of these scenes points at the same destination. Loki holds the multiverse together but his grip is slipping. Doom is exploiting the cracks — moving across realities, stealing power, approaching Franklin Richards, positioning himself to take the throne at the end of time. The Fantastic Four are chasing him. The X-Men have been pulled in through Monica's incursion. Sam Wilson has been warned. The New Avengers are watching the sky.

Avengers: Doomsday is not arriving without context. The MCU has been building this for years, across films, Disney+ series, and post-credits scenes most people forgot existed. When December 18, 2026 arrives, everything above will pay off in the same two and a half hours.
